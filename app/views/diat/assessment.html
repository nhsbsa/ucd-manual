Assessments at the NHSBSA

All NHSBSA services need either an NHSBSA or GDS assessment.

What happens at the assessment

NHSBSA assessments are peer reviews and build on the work your team is already doing with their professional communities.

They are run by a panel of 4 or 5 assessors, including a lead assessor, a user researcher, a designer and a technical lead.

At the assessment, you’ll need to:

  • give a brief overview of the service
  • show the end-to-end journey

You’ll then show how the service meets the Digital Service Standard by answering questions about:

  • user needs
  • design
  • technology
  • your team
  • analytics and key performance indicators

You don’t need to prepare extra material, you’ll be able to use materials you’ve already built for show and tells.

Getting your result

You’ll get your result within 5 working days of the assessment.

What happens next depends on the result of the assessment and the development phase your service is in.

How to book an assessment

Email DIaT@nhsbsa.nhs.net to find out which one you need and to book.
